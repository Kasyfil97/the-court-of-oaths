# The Court of Oaths — How to Play Guide

## 🎮 Quick Start

1. **Choose Your Class**: Select from Knight, Rogue, Merchant, or Spy
2. **Face Rivals**: Battle 1-5 opponents in a series of rounds
3. **Make Decisions**: Each turn, COOPERATE or BETRAY
4. **Win**: Be the first to meet one of four win conditions

---

## 🆕 NEW MECHANICS: Bot Learning & Economic Pressure

### 1. **Bot Learning & Adaptation** 🤖

Opponents **no longer follow predictable patterns**. They adapt to your playstyle in real-time.

#### How It Works:
- **The Saint** (Always Cooperate): 
  - Normally cooperates, but if you betray frequently (>40% of moves), they occasionally retaliate
  - Creates a risk: exploit too much and face unpredictable betrayals

- **The Mirror** (Tit-for-Tat):
  - Copies your moves, BUT tests your trust occasionally
  - If you've cooperated 5 turns in a row, they might betray to see if you're exploitable

- **The Grudge** (Holds grudges):
  - Remembers your first betrayal, BUT might rarely forgive after 5+ peaceful rounds
  - Creates tension: Do you risk reconciliation?

- **The Flatterer** (Manipulator):
  - Switches from cooperation to betrayal, but **speeds up if you betray first**
  - The switch point is randomized (2-4 turns) each game

- **The Serpent** (Always Betray):
  - Consistently betrays (as expected)

#### Strategy Implications:
- You can't memorize the optimal move sequence
- You must **adapt dynamically** during each game
- Betraying has real consequences that ripple across opponents
- Cooperation builds longer-term trust (but watch for exploitation)

---

### 2. **Economic Scarcity & Inflation** 💰

Gold is no longer abundant. The economy matters.

#### Changes:
| Aspect | Before | After | Why |
|--------|--------|-------|-----|
| Starting Gold | 30 | **20** | Scarcity creates tension |
| Economy Win | Gold ≥ 100 | **Gold ≥ 150** | Harder to achieve via wealth alone |
| Gold Multiplier | Fixed | **Variable (70%-100%)** | Inflation: More gold in play = less value |

#### The Inflation Mechanic:

When total gold in the game (player + all opponents) exceeds baseline (80 gold):
- **80-100 gold in play**: Normal value (1.0x multiplier)
- **100-120 gold in play**: Moderate inflation (0.85x multiplier)
- **120+ gold in play**: Severe inflation (0.7x multiplier)

**Example**:
- Normal Cooperate/Cooperate: Both gain +1 Gold
- With high inflation: Both gain +0.7 Gold (rounded)

#### Strategy Implications:
- **Don't hoard gold**. High inflation punishes accumulation
- **Diversify win conditions**. One path (gold) is harder now
- **Bankruptcy matters**. Forcing opponents to 0 gold is more valuable
- **Trust and Honor become competitive alternatives** to wealth

## 🏆 Four Win Conditions (Updated)

### 1. **Economy** 💎
**Goal**: Accumulate 150 gold

**Best For**: Merchant class (1.5x gold multiplier)

**Challenge**: Economic inflation makes this harder than before. Don't rely solely on passive gold gain.

---

### 2. **Domination** ⚔️
**Goal**: Eliminate ≥2 opponents (reduce their gold to ≤0)

**Best For**: Aggressive betrayers, Rogue class (+2 gold on betray)

**Challenge**: With bot adaptation, pure betrayal strategies fail. You must exploit opponents' weaknesses tactically.

---

### 3. **Diplomacy** 🤝
**Goal**: Gain ≥70 Trust with ≥3 opponents

**Best For**: Consistent cooperators, Knight class (+1 honor on cooperate)

**Challenge**: Maintaining trust across multiple opponents. One betrayal resets progress with that opponent.

---

### 4. **Honor Run** 👑
**Goal**: Never betray + 15+ turns + 60+ honor

**Best For**: Pacifist playstyle, exploiting the Knight class

**Challenge**: Requires discipline. Opponents will test your resolve; can you resist betraying back?

## 🎯 Gameplay Tips

### Against **The Saint**:
- Early cooperation builds rapport
- Occasional betrayal is forgiven
- **But**: Betray too much (>40%) and they fight back
- **Tip**: Use them for steady resource gain, but don't abuse their kindness

### Against **The Mirror**:
- They copy your moves, so you control the pattern
- **But**: If you're too predictable, they test you
- **Tip**: Mix cooperation and betrayal strategically, or lean hard into cooperation to trigger their rare betrayal for a surprise swing

### Against **The Grudge**:
- First betrayal matters; they hold grudges forever
- **But**: Rare forgiveness is possible after 5+ peaceful rounds
- **Tip**: Either never betray them OR betray early and accept eternal enmity

### Against **The Flatterer**:
- They cooperate first, then switch to betrayal
- **The switch point is randomized** (2-4 turns, different each game)
- **But**: If you betray, they switch immediately
- **Tip**: Cooperate to maximize the cooperation phase, or betray early if you see the pattern forming

### Against **The Serpent**:
- Always betrays, always
- **Tip**: Don't waste cooperation attempts. Minimize losses or focus on other opponents

## 💡 Advanced Strategy

### Multi-Path Approach
With economic pressure, no single path dominates:

1. **Economy + Domination**: Rogue class, target weak opponents for elimination while farming gold
2. **Diplomacy + Honor**: Knight class, build massive trust and honor reserves
3. **Mixed**: Merchant, exploit inflation mechanic and balance multiple win conditions

### Reading Bot Patterns
- **Early Game (Turns 1-5)**: Identify which archetype you're facing
- **Mid Game (Turns 6-12)**: Exploit their adaptation thresholds
- **Late Game (Turn 15+)**: Final sprint to your chosen win condition

### Economic Management
- Don't accumulate gold wastefully
- Force opponents to 0 to remove economic pressure
- Use high-inflation periods to dump gold through betrayals (they lose value anyway)

## ⚙️ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why is starting gold lower?**
A: Scarcity creates tension and forces strategic choices. You can't just farm gold mindlessly.

**Q: Why is the economy win harder now?**
A: To force diversification. All four win paths are now balanced; no single path dominates.

**Q: Why do bots adapt?**
A: To punish exploitative strategies and reward dynamic play. Every game feels different.

**Q: What's inflation and why does it matter?**
A: It's economic realism. More gold in circulation = less value per unit. Hoarding is punished.

**Q: Can I still win by betraying everything?**
A: Theoretically yes, but harder. Bots adapt to pure betrayal, and you'll face retaliation. Mix strategies.

**Q: Is the Knight class still best for pacifism?**
A: Yes, the +1 honor on cooperate bonus is now more valuable (with higher honor thresholds).
